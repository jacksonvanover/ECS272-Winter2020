This visualization shows the sleeping patterns of a newborn for the first 6 months of her life. Each revolution represents a single day with the light and dark portions representing the hours spent awake and asleep respectively. The top of the rings represents midnight. Each ring is to be read clockwise. The innermost ring is the first day of the child's life.

The visualization is static.

Examples of design choices include the use of colors, the unique coordinate axes, and the choice to omit legends or labels.

The rationale behind the design choices is given in the comments by the author of the visualization (who is the father of the child). He says, "...I settled on this design simply because my main goal was to cut this pattern into timber using a CNC router or laser and install a 24 hour clock mechanism into it and hang it in her room. So aesthetics were far more important to me than visual clarity."

While the visualization is beautiful, there is not a lot of depth to the information available to the viewer. We get something of an intuition of the larger picture but getting anything finer-grained is not easy.
